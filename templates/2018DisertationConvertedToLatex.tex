\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}

Joblessness is a major crisis in South Africa, both at a governmental and individual level. 
The unemployment rate has been steadily measured at approximately 25\% for the past five years from 2012.

This research adopts a bottom-up, people-centric approach using Co-design under the umbrella of Participatory Design (PD). 
We advocate that ICTs have strong potential to empower unemployed and underemployed individuals in marginalized communities and improve their social and economic wellbeing.

\chapter{Introduction}

This chapter introduces unemployment and the methodology used in this research.

Unemployment is a worldwide crisis affecting millions. 
In South Africa, the unemployment rate reached 26.7\% in 2016. 
The United Nations lists ``Decent Work and Economic Growth'' as Goal 8 of the Sustainable Development Goals.

\section{Research Question}

In this research, we sought to understand ICT setbacks for employment among the low-skilled.

\begin{itemize}
    \item In what ways are the low-skilled using existing ICTs to find jobs?
    \item What are the barriers to the use of ICTs for job searching?
    \item Which technologies are appropriate for low-skilled job seekers?
    \item What are the salient design requirements?
    \item What are the trade-offs when performing Co-design with marginalized individuals?
\end{itemize}
