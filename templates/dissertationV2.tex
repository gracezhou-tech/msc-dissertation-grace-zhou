\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}

This dissertation explores how co-design methodologies can be used to discern and overcome barriers to employment among unemployed youth in South Africa. Grounded in ICT4D and participatory design theory, the study employs a Tell–Make–Enact co-design cycle across three iterative phases: understanding unemployment challenges, prototyping a solution, and deploying a working system.

Through collaboration with job readiness programmes, including The Zanokhanyo Network and Afrika Tikkun, the research identifies structural, informational, and technological barriers that hinder employment access. The findings demonstrate how participatory approaches can surface hidden identity, hidden aptitude, and systemic constraints while supporting the development of a contextually appropriate job-notification and CV-generation platform.

The study contributes to ICT4D scholarship by highlighting the importance of sustained co-design processes in enabling human empowerment and sustainable technology interventions.
